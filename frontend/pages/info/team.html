Our Team

Specialists in fine and decorative arts

Expertise and Experience

Zauction is supported by a team of specialists with backgrounds in art history, museum curation, conservation, and auction house practice. Our cataloguers and evaluators bring decades of combined experience in their respective fields.

Each department is led by individuals with advanced training and professional credentials. We maintain relationships with independent experts, conservators, and academic institutions to ensure accurate attribution and assessment.

Departments

European Decorative Arts

Our specialists in European furniture, silver, ceramics, and glass have extensive knowledge of period styles, makers' marks, and regional characteristics. They evaluate condition, authenticity, and provenance for items ranging from the Renaissance through the early 20th century.

Fine Art

The fine art department handles paintings, works on paper, and sculpture. Specialists conduct research on attribution, examine technique and materials, and consult auction records and scholarly literature to establish estimates and authenticity.

Asian Art and Ceramics

Our Asian art specialists focus on Chinese, Japanese, and Southeast Asian ceramics, works of art, and decorative objects. They assess period, kiln attribution, condition, and cultural significance.

Rare Books and Manuscripts

The books and manuscripts department evaluates printed books, maps, documents, and ephemera. Specialists examine bindings, paper, printing techniques, and provenance to determine rarity and value.

Jewelry and Watches

Our jewelry specialists assess gemstones, precious metals, period settings, and maker's marks. They work with independent gemologists and watchmakers when detailed technical evaluation is required.

Ongoing Research

Our team maintains active research practices, consulting auction archives, museum collections, and scholarly publications. We attend professional conferences, visit exhibitions, and engage with the broader community of specialists and collectors.

This commitment to continuous learning ensures that our cataloguing remains accurate and our market assessments reflect current scholarship and collecting trends.
