Buyer's Guide

What you should know before bidding

Before You Bid

Carefully review the lot description, condition report, and photographs. Read the auction terms, including the buyer's premium percentage and payment deadline.

If you have questions about a lot, contact the appropriate specialist department before bidding. We can provide additional photographs, measurements, or clarification on condition issues.

Consider arranging a viewing if the lot is of significant value or if condition details are critical to your decision.

Understanding Estimates

Each lot includes a pre-sale estimate range (e.g., $2,000-$3,000). Estimates are based on recent auction results for comparable items, condition, rarity, and market trends.

Estimates are not guarantees. Final prices may fall below, within, or above the estimate range depending on bidder interest and competition.

Starting bids are typically set below the low estimate to encourage participation.

Condition and Authenticity

All lots are sold as-is. Condition reports describe known flaws, restorations, and wear. However, condition reports are not exhaustive. Buyers are responsible for examining lots and forming their own opinions.

We make every effort to accurately describe and authenticate items, but we do not guarantee attribution, age, or provenance unless explicitly stated. Independent expert opinions are recommended for high-value purchases.

Returns are accepted only in cases of material misrepresentation as described in our Terms of Service.

Buyer's Premium

A buyer's premium is added to your winning bid. The premium percentage (typically 20-25%) is stated in each auction's terms. This fee is standard in the auction industry and covers operational costs.

Your total purchase price is: Hammer Price (winning bid) + Buyer's Premium + Shipping + Taxes (if applicable).

Payment

Payment is due within 7 days of the auction closing. Accepted methods include bank transfer, credit card, and certified check. Payment details are provided in your winning notification email.

Lots will not be released until payment is received in full. Late payments may incur fees or result in account restrictions.

Shipping and Pickup

Buyers are responsible for arranging and paying for shipping or pickup. We can recommend professional art shippers and packers, but buyers are responsible for coordinating and paying these services directly.

Items must be collected or shipping arranged within 14 days of payment. Storage fees may apply for items left beyond this period.

International buyers are responsible for customs duties, taxes, and import regulations in their country.

Bidding Limits

New bidders or those bidding on high-value lots may be asked to provide additional verification or a deposit before bidding. This protects both buyers and sellers from fraudulent activity.

Bid limits may be applied to accounts with outstanding balances or previous payment issues.

Questions and Support

If you have questions about bidding, payment, or shipping, contact our support team at support@zauction.com. We are here to assist you throughout the process.
