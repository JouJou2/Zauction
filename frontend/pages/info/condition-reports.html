Condition Reports

How we assess and disclose condition

Purpose of Condition Reports

Condition reports provide detailed descriptions of an item's physical state, including wear, damage, restorations, and alterations. These reports help bidders make informed decisions and set appropriate expectations.

All lots include a condition statement in the catalogue description. More detailed reports are available upon request for lots of significant value or complexity.

What We Examine

Our specialists examine each item under appropriate lighting and, when necessary, with magnification. We assess:

  • Overall condition and structural integrity
  • Surface condition (scratches, stains, fading, discoloration)
  • Evidence of repair, restoration, or alteration
  • Completeness (missing parts, replaced elements)
  • Functionality (for mechanical objects)
  • Signs of age-appropriate wear versus damage

Disclosure Standards

We disclose all condition issues that materially affect value or usability. This includes cracks, chips, losses, repairs, repainting, replaced parts, and structural weaknesses.

Minor wear consistent with age and use may not be individually itemized but is acknowledged in general statements such as "minor surface wear" or "age-appropriate patina."

We do not guarantee that condition reports are exhaustive. Buyers are encouraged to examine items in person or request additional photographs of specific areas of concern.

Requesting Additional Information

If you require more detailed condition information, contact the specialist department handling the auction. We can provide:

  • Additional photographs (detail shots, reverse, base, interior)
  • Measurements and dimensions
  • Clarification on specific condition issues
  • Information about previous conservation or restoration

Requests should be made well in advance of the auction closing to allow time for examination and response.

Viewing Appointments

For high-value lots or items where condition is critical, we recommend arranging a viewing. In-person examination allows you to assess condition, scale, color, and quality more accurately than photographs permit.

Contact us to schedule a viewing appointment. We will accommodate your schedule whenever possible.

Limitations

Condition reports reflect the opinion of our specialists based on visual examination. We do not perform scientific testing, x-ray analysis, or destructive examination unless specifically noted.

Hidden defects, internal damage, or issues not visible to the naked eye may not be detected or reported. Buyers bid at their own risk and are responsible for their own due diligence.

Sold As-Is

All lots are sold as-is, where-is, with all faults. Condition reports are provided as a courtesy and do not constitute a warranty or guarantee.

Returns are accepted only in cases of material misrepresentation as defined in our Terms of Service. Normal wear, minor undisclosed flaws, or differences of opinion about condition do not constitute grounds for return.
