Authentication

Verification and expert review

Our Approach to Authentication

Authentication is the process of determining whether an object is genuine, correctly attributed, and from the period or maker claimed. We take authentication seriously and employ multiple methods to verify items before offering them for sale.

Our specialists have extensive training and experience in their fields. They examine items for signs of authenticity including materials, construction techniques, style, wear patterns, and provenance.

Examination Methods

Our authentication process includes:

  • Visual Examination: Assessment of style, technique, materials, and construction under appropriate lighting and magnification.
  • Provenance Research: Review of ownership history, exhibition records, auction archives, and published references.
  • Comparative Analysis: Comparison with documented examples in museum collections, scholarly publications, and auction records.
  • Technical Analysis: When appropriate, scientific testing such as thermoluminescence dating, x-ray fluorescence, or pigment analysis.
  • Expert Consultation: Consultation with independent specialists, conservators, and academic authorities.

Independent Experts

For items of significant value or uncertain attribution, we consult independent experts and recognized authorities. Third-party authentication reports from established experts or laboratories are obtained when appropriate.

Costs for independent authentication are typically borne by the consignor. Buyers may request additional expert opinions at their own expense.

Catalogue Descriptions

Our catalogue descriptions reflect our best judgment based on available evidence. We use specific terminology to indicate levels of certainty:

  • "By [Artist/Maker]": In our opinion, the work is by the named artist or maker.
  • "Attributed to [Artist/Maker]": Probably by the named artist or maker, but some uncertainty exists.
  • "Circle of [Artist/Maker]": By an unidentified artist working closely with or under the influence of the named artist.
  • "Manner of [Artist/Maker]": In the style of the named artist but of a later date.
  • "After [Artist/Maker]": A copy of a known work by the named artist.

These terms follow established auction house conventions and indicate the degree of confidence in attribution.

Limitations and Disclaimers

Authentication is not an exact science. Opinions may differ among experts, and new information may emerge that changes our understanding of an object.

We do not guarantee attribution, age, or authenticity unless explicitly stated in writing. Our catalogue descriptions represent our opinion based on examination and research at the time of cataloguing.

Buyers are encouraged to conduct their own research and seek independent expert opinions, particularly for high-value purchases.

Reattribution and New Information

If significant new information emerges after cataloguing that materially affects attribution or authenticity, we will notify registered bidders and may withdraw the lot from sale.

Buyers who discover new information after purchase may contact us. We will review the evidence and, if warranted, facilitate consultation with experts or consider a return under our Terms of Service.

Forgeries and Fakes

We reject items we believe to be forgeries, reproductions, or misrepresented. However, sophisticated forgeries may evade detection.

If an item is later proven to be a forgery or materially misrepresented, buyers may be entitled to a refund under the terms outlined in our Terms of Service. Claims must be supported by credible expert opinion and made within a reasonable timeframe.
